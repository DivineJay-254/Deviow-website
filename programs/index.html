Our Programs

Holistic support for dignity and self-reliance

DEVIOW integrates livelihood empowerment, financial inclusion, GBV prevention, and child wellbeing so every woman and girl can thrive.

Each program is designed with women and girls at the center, addressing root causes of poverty and violence while building sustainable pathways to dignity and economic independence.

Our integrated approach means participants benefit from multiple touchpoints—skills, savings, protection, and wellbeing—creating a comprehensive support system for lasting transformation.

Tailoring & Dressmaking

Economic independence through practical skills

Many women in Kakuma have limited access to employment opportunities. Through tailoring and dressmaking training, we help participants gain practical skills that become a sustainable source of income.

What Participants Learn

  • Basic and advanced tailoring techniques
  • Garment cutting and stitching
  • Repair and alteration work
  • Customer handling and pricing strategies
  • Basic entrepreneurship and financial management

The goal is not only training. The goal is economic independence for women and their families.

GBV Prevention & Response

Safe spaces and community strength

Community Awareness Dialogue

Women and girls continue to face various forms of violence, discrimination, and social exclusion. This program focuses on creating safety, building community accountability, and supporting survivors.

Program Activities

  • Community awareness and education campaigns
  • Safe spaces for women and girls to gather
  • Referral pathways for survivors seeking support
  • Community sensitization on rights and protection
  • Promoting dignity and accountability

We strengthen awareness while empowering communities to become part of the solution to end violence.

Financial Literacy & VSLA Groups

Building financial resilience together

Income without strategic financial planning often remains unstable and unreliable. Through financial literacy training and Village Savings and Loan Association (VSLA) groups, women learn to manage money effectively and build household security.

What Women Learn

  • Saving practices and financial discipline
  • Budgeting and expense tracking
  • Loan management and ethical repayment
  • Small business planning and growth
  • Group accountability and collective strength

This program helps participants build economic resilience and reduce dependency on unstable income sources.

Play & Grow

Safe, nurturing spaces for children's growth

Play & Grow Community Session

Many children in Kakuma face isolation, stress, and limited opportunities for holistic development. Using play therapy, creative activities, and structured mentorship, we create supportive spaces where all children can thrive emotionally and socially.

Program Components

  • Confidence building through creative play
  • Life skills and social-emotional development
  • Mentorship from trained community facilitators
  • Sports, arts, and games for holistic growth
  • Safe spaces for peer support and belonging

We believe growth encompasses more than academics. Emotional wellbeing, confidence, and joy are essential foundations for thriving.
