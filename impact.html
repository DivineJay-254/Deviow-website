Highlights from PRODEVIOW’s journey

Trained over 75 women in financial literacy and business management.

VSLA growth

Established 4 active VSLA groups improving women's financial inclusion.

Tailoring & dressmaking

Equipped 25 women with tailoring skills now supplying garments to local markets.

Safe spaces

Created safe spaces for GBV survivors to access support and peer healing.

Community advocacy

Conducted 22 community awareness campaigns on women's rights, protection, and empowerment.

Community voice

“I can provide for my daughters and mentor others.”

Asha joined the Livelihoods Program after leaving an abusive relationship. Today she runs a tailoring business, mentors newcomers, and keeps her daughters in school through VSLA savings.

Her story mirrors dozens of PRODEVIOW graduates who pair economic skills with leadership roles to protect others from violence.

Graduates mentor the next cohort of trainees

How we measure change

Trust, data, and accountability drive our learning loops

Survivor-centered data

Consent-based case tracking ensures women choose what information is collected and how it is used to improve services.

Community scorecards

Quarterly dialogues gather feedback from youth, men, and elders on safety, prevention, and service quality.

Partner coordination

We align with humanitarian actors to prevent duplication, close referral gaps, and advocate for systemic change.
