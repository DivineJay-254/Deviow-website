Who we are

Defying Violence Against Women

DEVIOW was founded in 2020 in Kakuma Refugee Camp by women who survived inequality and gender-based violence. Today we remain refugee-led and community-driven—standing as a beacon of hope, resilience, and empowerment across Kakuma and Kalobeyei.

About Us

DEVIOW (Defying Violence Against Women) is a refugee-led, community-based organization operating in Kakuma Refugee Camp and Kalobeyei Integrated Settlement, Turkana County, Kenya. Founded in 2020 and formally registered in 2026, DEVIOW addresses critical challenges facing women, girls, and youth in displacement—particularly gender-based violence (GBV), economic exclusion, and limited access to education.

What began as a small grassroots initiative has grown into a structured organization delivering integrated, community-driven programs that combine protection, skills development, and economic empowerment. Our holistic approach ensures every woman and girl has the opportunity to live with dignity, self-reliance, and purpose. When women rise, communities thrive.

Our Approach

  • Community-led and survivor-centered approach grounded in lived experience.
  • Integration of protection with economic empowerment for long-term resilience.
  • Practical, locally relevant programs with strong community ownership.
  • Survivor leadership directing solutions that reflect camp and settlement realities.

Our Vision

A society where women and girls live free from violence, with dignity, equal opportunities, and sustainable livelihoods.

Our Mission

To empower vulnerable women, girls, and youth through practical skills training, financial inclusion, education, and GBV prevention enabling them to become self-reliant and resilient.

Our Core Values

  • Sisterhood

  • Community Power
