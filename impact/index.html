Our achievements

Highlights from PRODEVIOW's journey

🎓

Financial literacy transformation

Trained over 75 women in financial literacy and business management, empowering them to make strategic decisions about money.

💰

VSLA growth & trust

Established 4 active VSLA groups improving women's financial inclusion and creating safe spaces for collective savings.

✂️

Livelihoods launched

Equipped 25 women with tailoring skills now supplying garments to local markets and generating sustainable income.

🛡️

Safe spaces created

Built safe spaces for GBV survivors to access support, peer healing, and pathways to justice and dignity.

🗣️

Community mobilization

Conducted 22 community awareness campaigns on women's rights, protection, and empowerment reaching thousands.

👥

Mentor networks

Program graduates now mentor newcomers, creating self-sustaining cycles of empowerment and peer support.

Community Voices

Real stories of transformation

"I can provide for my daughters and mentor others."

Asha

After leaving an abusive relationship, Asha joined the Livelihoods Program. Today she runs a tailoring business, keeps her daughters in school through VSLA savings, and mentors other women rebuilding their lives.

"Now I'm teaching my sisters about money."

Grace

Grace completed financial literacy training and joined a VSLA group. She now teaches her community about budgeting, savings, and economic independence— multiplying impact across her network.

"Protection is a community responsibility."

Community Leader Moses

After attending PRODEVIOW awareness campaigns, community leaders like Moses began openly discussing GBV and accountability, shifting norms within their households and neighborhoods.

How we measure change

Trust, data, and accountability drive our learning loops

Survivor-centered data

Consent-based case tracking ensures women choose what information is collected and how it is used to improve services.

Community scorecards

Quarterly dialogues gather feedback from youth, men, and elders on safety, prevention, and service quality.

Partner coordination

We align with humanitarian actors to prevent duplication, close referral gaps, and advocate for systemic change.
